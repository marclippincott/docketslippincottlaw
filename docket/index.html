Import deadlines from Outlook (.ics)

📋 How to export your Outlook calendar to .ics
  1. Open Outlook (desktop app or Outlook.com)
  2. Click File → Open & Export → Import/Export (desktop)
    — or on Outlook.com: Settings → View all Outlook settings → Calendar → Shared calendars → Publish
  3. Choose "Export to a file" → "iCalendar format (.ics)"
  4. Select your Calendar (the one with your case deadlines)
  5. Set the date range: today through 12 months from now
  6. Save the file and upload it below
Tip: Both Marc and Erika should export and upload their calendars separately to catch all deadlines. Each upload is independent.

Search syntax

The search box supports Lexis/Westlaw-style boolean syntax. Searches run across debtor names, file numbers, addresses, loan accounts, MHP names, reasons for default, client & counsel notes, counsel info, and all journal entry text.

Operators
ANDBoth terms must appear. Default if you just type two words.
OREither term may appear.
NOTExcludes results containing the term.
"phrase"Match the exact phrase (case-insensitive).
( )Group terms to control order of evaluation.
Examples
bankruptcyAny matter mentioning "bankruptcy"
demand letterBoth words must appear
"land plus"Exact phrase
repossession OR foreclosureEither term
default NOT curedHas "default" but not "cured"
(payoff OR reinstatement) AND receivedUse parens to group
"sheriff sale" AND NOT cancelledCombine phrase, AND, NOT
Operators are case-sensitive — use uppercase AND / OR / NOT. Lowercase and/or/not are treated as ordinary search words. Other filters (client, status, rep, type) still apply on top of the search.

Statute of limitations

Computed expiration
Texas: 4-year SOL on written contracts (CPRC § 16.004) from accrual; for installment notes accrual is typically the date of default. Acceleration, partial payment, or written acknowledgment may shift accrual. Real-property liens have a separate 4-year limit (§ 16.035). The computed date is a guide, not legal advice.
